Spam Mail
Classification

Spam mail classification is the task of automatically sorting and labeling incoming emails as either 'spam' or 'non-spam' (ham). It is a critical component of email filtering systems. This process involves analyzing email content, sender information, and other factors to detect and block unwanted and potentially harmful emails. By employing sophisticated algorithms and machine learning techniques, spam mail classification helps keep users' inboxes free from unsolicited, fraudulent, or malicious messages, ensuring a more secure and efficient email experience.
